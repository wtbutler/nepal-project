Glossary

Kimerer LaMothe: “[A]ny definition of religion must be recognized as inherently dynamic ... a dynamic definition moves across the distinction of insider versus outsider: something may appear as ‘religion’ to either. Religion ... exists in the moment of its performances as a kind of doing that embodies a person in relation to a sense of the world – a sense that may be represented in relation to a tradition or as a belief in an Other. The relevant question for studying religion becomes: how and why and to what end does some phenomenon have meaning for someone as the terms or conditions of his embodiment”
- (Between Dancing and Writing: The Practice of Religious Studies, 2004, 248).
